The System Evolution: From Thymaravumkara to the Cloud
01. The Seed of Curiosity (2007)
My journey didn't start in a high-tech lab; it started in Thymaravumkara, a small village in Thiruvalla, Pathanamthitta. I grew up in a family where financial resources were modest, but intellectual curiosity was abundant.
While others played, I read. I devoured newspapers—especially Padippura by Malayala Manorama—and sneaked into my older cousins' textbooks to read about subjects years ahead of my grade. In 2007, at age 11, I saw a computer for the first time at a neighbor’s house. I didn't just see a machine; I saw a mystery. I was obsessed with understanding how so much logic could be packed into a single box.
02. The "Computer Genie"
By the 5th standard, I finally got my hands on computers at school, toggling between Windows and Ubuntu (IT@School OS). What started as gaming quickly turned into a deep-seated reputation. By the 9th standard, I had emerged as the "Computer Genie" of my school, eventually serving as the Secretary/President of the IT Club.
Despite this, the path forward wasn't clear. Based on a teacher’s suggestion, I followed the "safe" route: VHSE in Office Secretaryship, followed by a B.Com in Computer Applications at SN College, Thiruvalla.
03. The Survival Grind & The Mismatch
Life after graduation was a lesson in grit. To support myself and my family, I took whatever work came my way:
Apprentice Tile Worker: Learning the physical labor of construction.
SIM Promoter: Selling Reliance Communications connections on the street.
Accountant: My first "professional" job at Achhoz Light & Glass Zone, Vallamkulam.
I was earning ₹15,000 a month, but I felt trapped. Sitting behind a ledger, I realized I wasn't born to manage accounts—I was born to build systems. I saved every rupee I could, resigned, and moved to Perinthalmanna to study at the SISCO Institute of Smartphone Technology.
04. The Hardware Soul (2019 – 2021)
I went back to the guts of the machine. I moved over 2,000 kilometers away to Jagdalpur, Chhattisgarh, to work at Mobile Guru. There, I mastered the art of smartphone hardware and software diagnostics. I wasn't just fixing screens; I was flashing ROMs, fixing IMEI logic, and understanding the bridge between silicon and code.
In December 2021, I returned to SISCO—not as a student, but as a Faculty Member. For nearly four years, I taught hundreds of students the intricacies of smartphone software repair.
05. The Pivot to Logic (2022 – 2025)
While teaching hardware, my mind shifted toward automation. In 2022, I faced a repetitive problem at the institute and solved it with my first piece of real code: a Python-based OCR tool using Tesseract to extract phone numbers from screenshots into CSV files.
That small script changed everything. I realized that while fixing a phone helps one person, building a system helps thousands. On August 12, 2025, I resigned from my teaching post to commit fully to software engineering. I spent my time upskilling, diving deep into AI, Next.js, and automation.
06. The Present: Building Control
On October 9, 2025, I joined Zabnix Private Limited. I started in IT Support, but my nature as a "Vibe Coder" quickly pushed me into a Developer role. Today, I build internal systems for my company while developing independent products for clients and friends in my free time.
My Philosophy
I am an ambivert who talks to servers more than people. I am an atheist and anti-pseudoscience; I trust logs, evidence, and stack traces over "gut feelings."
From a tile worker's apprentice in a small village to a Systems Engineer architecting digital solutions, my mission remains the same: Replacing manual chaos with automated control.
